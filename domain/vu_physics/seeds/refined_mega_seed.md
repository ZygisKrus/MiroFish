Life in Saulėtekis is a delicate balance between the brutalist architecture of the Physics Faculty (FF) and the chaotic energy of the dormitories. The **"Kamčiatka" (Kampos)** and **"Niujorkas"** dorms are the social hubs where the true decisions are made. 

The **Physics "Swarm"** is currently obsessed with the legend of **Prof. Vičas**, whose Calculus exams are the primary filter for first-year survival. If you haven't cried in the 'Didžioji Fizikos Auditorija', you aren't a real VU physicist yet.


The **MKIC (Mokslinės komunikacijos ir informacijos centras)** is the 24/7 battleground. It's where students silently judge each other's study methods. Seeing someone smoothly scrolling through Fizkonspektas's dark mode on an iPad here creates immense FOMO. Meanwhile, the **NFT (Nacionalinis Fizinių ir Technologijos Mokslų Centras)** labs are where the real stress happens, and where the app's quick-reference capabilities are most desperately needed under the table.


## The Economic Friction
The average student budget is calculated in **"Jammi Kebabs"**. A standard kebab costs around €5-6. When **Fizkonspektas** launched at **the subscription fee**, the immediate reaction was: *"That's two kebabs. Is an AI tutor worth two Jammi runs?"* This price point has created a rift:
- **The Academic Purists** (like Rūta Meilutytė) believe the app is a shortcut to a 'skola' later in life.
- **The Social Survivors** (led by Mantas Kalnietis and Viltė Kazlauskaitė) are already mapping out 'cliques' to share account costs.

## Tribal Rituals
- **FiDi (Fiziko Diena):** The most important day of the year. If the app can help students pass their labs before FiDi so they can actually party, it wins.
- **Laborai (Labs):** The primary source of 'ataskaitos' (reports). The app's KaTeX renderer is a major draw here, as manually typing formulas in LaTeX is the #1 cause of sleep deprivation in Saulėtekis.

## BETTA-FISH STRATEGIC INJECTIONS (THE SUBSCRIPTION TRAP)

Based on the updated strategic objective to maximize the "Subscription Trap" by making students feel like they are outsmarting the system, I've synthesized three new distinct test scenarios. 

**Crucial Constraint:** The app uses Google OAuth. Account sharing is *technically* allowed, but requires sharing a personal Gmail (unsafe) or creating a shared dummy Gmail (high friction).

SCENARIO A - "The Crammer's Exploit"
- Slogan: "Nulaužk sesiją per 7 dienas" (Hack the exam session in 7 days)
- Pricing: €7.99/month individual plan.
- Free Trial: 7 Days.
- Content Format: "Rapid Cheatsheet" (Stripped down: just the KaTeX formula and a one-sentence 'Prasmė', optimized for last-minute cramming).
- UI Focus: Ultra-minimalist layout, high-contrast dark mode for easy reading on phones outside the lecture hall.
- Target Trap: Students take the 7-day trial to cram for a 'kolis'. Because €7.99 is relatively cheap, and creating dummy Gmails for a 1-week exploit is annoying, they use their personal Gmail and simply forget to cancel.

SCENARIO B - "The Phantom Loophole"
- Slogan: "10 dienų VIP fizikos archyvas" (10 days VIP physics archive)
- Pricing: the subscription feenth individual plan.
- Free Trial: 10 Days.
- Content Format: "Medium-Detailed" (The current standard: Intuicija, Matematika, Prasmė).
- UI Focus: "Industrial Archive Theme" (Anti-Halation Brutalism, JetBrains Mono, glassmorphism modals). Feels premium, underground, and exclusive.
- Target Trap: 10 days spans over a weekend and a half—the perfect duration to build a study habit and lose track of the cancellation date. Students feel they found a "loophole" with the weird 10-day number, falling into the monthly recurring charge.

SCENARIO C - "The Premium Cartel"
- Slogan: "Fizikos elito įrankis" (The tool of the physics elite)
- Pricing: €12.99/month individual plan.
- Free Trial: 14 Days.
- Content Format: "Deep-Dive Archive" (Heavy, textbook-replacement style, full rigorous mathematical proofs).
- UI Focus: Complex layout with sidebars, advanced search capabilities, and heavy dark-mode aesthetics. Feels like an expensive enterprise research tool.
- Target Trap: The high price makes the 14-day trial feel incredibly valuable. Students form "Cartels" by creating a dummy Gmail to share the account, thinking they are beating a €12.99 system. Because 14 days is so long, the 'cartel leader' forgets to cancel and gets hit with the premium charge.
