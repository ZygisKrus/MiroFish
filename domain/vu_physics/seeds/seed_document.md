# Fizkonspektas: Platform Launch at Vilnius University Physics Faculty

## 1. Product Overview: Fizkonspektas
Fizkonspektas is a premium, modern web application designed specifically for university-level physics students. It provides interactive, highly structured study notes (konspektai) covering complex topics like Electrodynamics, Quantum Mechanics, and Higher Mathematics.

### Key Features
*   **Interactive Notes**: Deep, step-by-step derivations of physics formulas rendered perfectly using KaTeX. The UI features an "Apple-style" glassmorphism design with dark mode support.
*   **AI Physics Assistant**: A GPT-4 powered chatbot integrated directly into the notes. It understands the specific context of the page the student is reading and answers complex physics questions, acting as a personal tutor.
*   **Pricing Model**: Students can access the platform via a 7-day free trial. After that, it costs €9.99/month for a subscription, or they can purchase lifetime "All Access."

## 2. Target Audience & Environment: Vilnius University (VU) Physics Faculty
The primary target audience is the student body at the Vilnius University (VU) Physics Faculty, located in the Saulėtekis campus.

### Student Personas & Pain Points
*   **First-Year Students (Freshmen)**: Overwhelmed by the transition from high school to university physics. They struggle heavily with Higher Mathematics and mechanics. They are highly active on social media and looking for any advantage to pass their exams.
*   **Third-Year Students**: Dealing with incredibly complex subjects like Quantum Mechanics and Statistical Physics. They are skeptical of generic AI tools (like ChatGPT) because it often hallucinates advanced math. They need precise, reliable derivations.
*   **The Skeptics**: Traditional students who prefer physical books and handwritten notes. They believe digital tools are a distraction and complain about the €9.99/month price tag, arguing that education should be free.
*   **The Tech Optimists**: Students who embrace AI. They love the AI Assistant feature because it saves them hours of trying to understand professor's vague lecture slides.

### The Ecosystem
*   **Professors**: Demanding academic staff who expect rigorous understanding of theory. Some professors warn students against using AI to cheat, while younger faculty members are curious about new educational tech.
*   **Saulėtekis Dormitories (Kamčiatka)**: The epicenter of student life. Word-of-mouth spreads rapidly here. If a tool helps someone pass a difficult midterm, the whole floor knows about it by evening.
*   **Online Communities**: 
    *   **Facebook Groups**: "VU Fizikos Fakultetas Studentai" (official announcements and serious discussions).
    *   **Reddit**: `r/lithuania` and specific student subreddits where people complain about exam difficulty and share survival tips.

## 3. The Launch Strategy (The Simulation Variables)
Fizkonspektas is launching with a multi-pronged marketing approach to see what resonates best with the VU Physics community:

*   **The Guerrilla Campaign (Saulėtekis Stickers)**: Placing QR code stickers with provocative slogans ("Failing Quantum Mechanics? Scan this.") on doors and notice boards in the Saulėtekis dorms.
*   **Digital Ads (Instagram)**: Running targeted Instagram ads showing the AI Assistant easily solving a complex electrodynamics problem.
*   **The "Free Trial" Hook**: Pushing the 7-day free trial right before the mid-term exam period, hoping students rely on it so much they subscribe afterward.

## 4. The Core Conflict (Simulation Catalyst)
As the platform launches, a debate erupts in the student community. Tech optimists claim Fizkonspektas is the ultimate cheat code for passing difficult exams, praising the AI Assistant. Skeptics and some professors argue it's too expensive and makes students lazy, preventing them from doing the hard mathematical derivations themselves. Meanwhile, the mid-term exams are approaching, and desperation is rising.
